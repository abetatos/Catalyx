### 9. `semiconductors_foundry` — composite 66.6 (rank #9)

**Catalysts driving alignment:** `struct_ai_capex_supercycle` is the sole active catalyst (base
catalyst_alignment 89.9, dominant structural). The catalyst chain is direct: hyperscaler AI
capex (~$87B/quarter combined as of Q1 2026) routes into Nvidia Blackwell and Google TPU
wafer orders that only TSMC's N3/N4 nodes can physically manufacture. TSMC Q1 2026 results
confirm the mechanism is live: $35.9B revenue (+35.1% YoY), 66.2% gross margin (near
historical peak = capacity-constrained pricing), and 2nm mass production commenced H1 2026
with 90,000–100,000 wafers/month already sold out for the year. No contradicting catalyst is
active. The 89.9 raw alignment is the ceiling of what `struct_ai_capex_supercycle` currently
produces — the sector cannot score higher than its primary catalyst permits. The composite
drops to 66.6 only because crowding risk (75, `narrative_maturity = crowded`) applies a
meaningful penalty: the foundry AI narrative has been mainstream-to-crowded since late 2024
and is now in every sell-side AI infrastructure report from Goldman, JPM, and Morgan Stanley.
Momentum score (78.4) is strong but below the catalyst signal, consistent with a crowded
thesis where prices have largely pre-run the fundamental catalyst.

**Non-obvious finding:** The market has correctly priced the AI capex tailwind into TSMC
(~$1.88T market cap, P/E >25x, 19/19 analyst buy ratings) but has not yet priced the
asymmetry between two simultaneous developments that are pulling in opposite directions.

On the de-risking side: TSMC Arizona Fab 21 Phase 2 begins equipment move-in Q3 2026,
targeting N3 HVM in 2027. CoWoS advanced packaging is being co-located in Arizona, meaning
TSMC will offer customers a complete AI chip supply chain on US soil — wafer + packaging —
for the first time. When that milestone is confirmed (expected H2 2026–H1 2027), the
geopolitical risk premium embedded in TSM's valuation should compress structurally, delivering
a re-rating event even without any earnings revision. This de-risking has not happened yet and
is not priced into consensus targets (~$467).

On the concentration risk side: the market treats Taiwan geopolitical risk as a volatility
factor that mean-reverts (precedent: Pelosi August 2022 — TSM ADR fell 25–35% in 3 days,
full recovery in 60 days). The non-consensus view is that the 2nm/CoWoS production ramp is
compressing the window during which a conflict-induced disruption could be absorbed by
alternative suppliers. Intel 18A is not at HVM yield parity. Samsung 3nm GAA yield recovery
is not complete. The gap between "TSMC is irreplaceable today" and "alternatives exist" is
widening, not narrowing, through 2026 — which means a prolonged disruption scenario becomes
more catastrophic over time, not less. The market's flat-premium treatment of this tail risk
understates its severity as TSMC's monopoly deepens.

Net: the alpha opportunity is not in the base AI capex case (crowded, priced). It lies in
monitoring the Arizona N3 HVM confirmation as a positive de-risking catalyst (~H2 2026), and
in sizing the position with an explicit geopolitical stop-loss protocol rather than assuming
the Pelosi-2022 recovery template will repeat at current TSMC scale and node dependency.
