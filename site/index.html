Entry timing

The execution window for every sector — when to enter a position you've already decided on (by fundamentals or as a dislocation opportunity). Separate from the composite: it never moves a score, only flags whether now is tense. Recommend-only.

opportunity What is an opportunity?

A sector flagged by the dislocation engine: it fell hard but its fundamentals are still intact (regime intact) and its catalyst is still confirmed, and the drop is mostly contagion — dragged down by the market (β·market), not by anything specific to its thesis. In other words: "the tide sold it, the thesis didn't break" → a panic dip worth buying. This is distinct from timing: dislocation says whether it's worth it; timing (the columns here) says when to enter. That's why they sit at the top with a green border — but even as an opportunity, the verdict can still say wait if it hasn't stabilized yet.

Default order: opportunities first, then the rest by tension. Click any column to re-sort. Verdict: enter now = calm, no objection · scale-in = a dip that's turning (enter in tranches) · wait — unstable = a falling knife, no base yet · wait — event = a near-term catalyst (event_date) could move it. A ⚠ overhang is that discrete event.

📐 Theoretical exercise

These books are hypothetical: you "buy" each strategy's recommendations at inception (the same Friday I opened my first real position, 2026-06-05) and the weights re-balance to the recommendation each run. No real buy/sell prices, no fees, no taxes — just NAV vs SPY from day 0. SPY is fetched and the whole curve is persisted run by run. The live curve grows one market day at a time; until it has ≥2 points each book is marked accruing and shows the hypothetical backtest for reference.
